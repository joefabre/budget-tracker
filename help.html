Getting Started

Welcome to Personal Budget Tracker

This application helps you track your income and expenses across different categories throughout the year. It's designed to be 508 compliant and accessible to all users.

Quick Start: Begin by entering your income sources in the Revenue section, then add your expenses in the appropriate categories. The summary cards will automatically update to show your financial status.

First Steps

1 Set Your Year: Use the year selector to choose the budget year you want to work with.
2 Add Income: Enter your monthly income amounts in the Revenue section.
3 Add Expenses: Fill in your monthly expenses across all categories.
4 Review Summary: Check the summary cards to see your overall financial picture.
5 Save Your Work: Click "Save Data" to store your budget information locally.

How-To Guides

Managing Income and Expenses

Adding New Income Sources

1 Navigate to the Revenue section
2 Click the "+ Add Income Source" button
3 Enter a descriptive name for the income source
4 Fill in monthly amounts across the year

Adding Expense Items

1 Choose the appropriate category (Home, Daily Living, etc.)
2 Click the "+ Add [Category] Expense" button
3 Enter a descriptive name for the expense
4 Input monthly amounts throughout the year

Deleting Items

Warning: Deleting items cannot be undone. Make sure to save your data before making changes.
1 Locate the item you want to remove
2 Click the "Delete" button in the Actions column
3 Confirm the deletion in the popup dialog

Data Management

Saving Your Budget

  • Click "Save Data" to store your budget in your browser's local storage
  • Data is automatically saved every 30 seconds while you work
  • Each year's data is saved separately

Loading Saved Data

1 Select the desired year from the dropdown
2 Click "Load Data" to retrieve saved information

Exporting Data

  • CSV Export: Creates a spreadsheet-compatible file
  • JSON Export: Creates a structured data file for backup
  • Print: Generates a printer-friendly version

Using Charts and Analytics

Viewing Charts

1 Click "View Charts" in the top controls
2 Browse through the three chart types
3 Close with the X button or Escape key

Chart Types Explained

  • Monthly Income vs Expenses: Bar chart comparing income and expenses month by month
  • Expense Breakdown: Pie chart showing percentage distribution across categories
  • Savings Trend: Line chart displaying your savings trajectory over time

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Tab - Navigate between form fields
  • Enter - Activate buttons and links
  • Escape - Close chart modal
  • Ctrl+P - Print budget (same as Print Budget button)
